# EB-2 NIW Case Strength Decision Tree

Use this decision tree to assess your case strength across the three Dhanasar pillars and determine your next steps.

---

## Section 1: Substantial Merit Assessment

**Question 1.1: Third-Party Evidence**

Do you have clear third-party evidence of your accomplishment and impact? (Publications, awards, citations, media coverage, patents)

- **YES, clear and substantial** (multiple categories, quantifiable metrics) → Go to 1.2
- **YES, but limited** (1-2 categories, modest metrics) → Go to 1.2
- **NOT REALLY** (mostly job titles and internal roles, minimal external validation) → **RED FLAG: Substantial merit is weak**

---

**Question 1.2: Publication Quality (if applicable)**

If you have publications, are they in recognized, peer-reviewed venues with reasonable impact?

- **YES, high-impact journals** (IF >3, top-tier in your field) → Go to 1.3
- **YES, moderate-impact journals** (IF 1-3, established venues) → Go to 1.3
- **NO PUBLICATIONS** (but other strong evidence like patents or awards) → Go to 1.3
- **WEAK VENUES** (predatory journals, low-impact outlets) → **RED FLAG: Publication quality undermines credibility**

---

**Question 1.3: Citation Impact (if applicable)**

If you have publications, do they have meaningful citation counts?

- **YES, high citations** (50+, especially with high h-index) → **SUBSTANTIAL MERIT: STRONG**
- **YES, moderate citations** (10-50) → **SUBSTANTIAL MERIT: MODERATE**
- **LOW or NO CITATIONS** (fewer than 10) → **SUBSTANTIAL MERIT: WEAK to MODERATE**
- **N/A, no publications** (but strong awards, patents, or other evidence) → Assess based on strength of other evidence

---

## Section 2: National Importance Assessment

**Question 2.1: Documented Need**

Can you cite published research, government reports, or credible sources documenting a recognized need in your area?

- **YES, clear published evidence** (government reports, peer-reviewed research, industry analyses) → Go to 2.2
- **YES, but limited sources** (1-2 sources) → Go to 2.2
- **NOT REALLY** (mostly logical inference or opinion) → **RED FLAG: National importance is weak. This is the #1 RFE trigger**

---

**Question 2.2: Connection Between Need and Your Work**

Does your specific work directly address the documented need?

- **YES, direct and clear connection** (no ambiguity about how your work solves the documented problem) → Go to 2.3
- **YES, reasonable connection** (connection is there but requires explanation) → Go to 2.3
- **INDIRECT or UNCLEAR** (your work is tangentially related but not specifically addressing the need) → **NATIONAL IMPORTANCE: WEAK**

---

**Question 2.3: Scope and Beneficiaries**

Does your work affect a significant population, sector, or important problem?

- **YES, broad scope** (affects professionals, organizations, or entire sectors) → **NATIONAL IMPORTANCE: STRONG**
- **YES, moderate scope** (affects a meaningful population but specialized/niche) → **NATIONAL IMPORTANCE: MODERATE**
- **LIMITED SCOPE** (affects only a small group or narrow community) → **NATIONAL IMPORTANCE: WEAK**

---

## Section 3: Execution Capacity Assessment

**Question 3.1: Proposed Endeavor Clarity**

Is your Proposed Endeavor clear, specific, and measurable?

- **YES, very clear** (specific objectives, measurable outcomes, realistic timeline, all documented) → Go to 3.2
- **YES, somewhat clear** (objectives are there, but timeline or outcomes could be more specific) → Go to 3.2
- **VAGUE or MISSING** (not clear what you're committing to, no timeline, no deliverables) → **RED FLAG: Execution capacity is weak**

---

**Question 3.2: Institutional Affiliation and Resource Commitment**

Do you have a clear institutional home and documented resource commitment?

- **YES, confirmed** (letter of support from employer/university, salary commitment, or resource allocation confirmed) → Go to 3.3
- **YES, preliminary** (university is interested, preliminary conversations, but not yet formalized) → Go to 3.3
- **NOT SECURED** ("I hope to find a position" or "I will apply to universities") → **RED FLAG: Execution capacity is weak**

---

**Question 3.3: Continuity with Your History**

Does your Proposed Endeavor build directly on your proven track record?

- **YES, direct continuation** (you've been doing this work; you're proposing more of the same) → **EXECUTION CAPACITY: STRONG**
- **YES, related continuation** (related to past work, skills transfer clearly demonstrated) → **EXECUTION CAPACITY: MODERATE to STRONG**
- **PIVOT or UNCLEAR** (significant departure from past work without clear justification) → **EXECUTION CAPACITY: WEAK to MODERATE**

---

## Section 4: Overall Case Classification

**Using your assessments above, find your profile:**

### Profile A: STRONG Case
- Substantial Merit: STRONG
- National Importance: STRONG
- Execution Capacity: STRONG

**Recommendation:** File with high confidence. Low RFE risk.

---

### Profile B: MODERATE Case (Majority Strong)
- At least 2 pillars STRONG, 1 MODERATE
- OR all 3 pillars MODERATE

**Recommendation:** File with reasonable confidence. Prepare for targeted RFE. Likely approvable.

---

### Profile C: MODERATE Case (Mixed Strength)
- 1 pillar STRONG, 2 pillars WEAK
- OR 2 pillars MODERATE, 1 WEAK

**Recommendation:** Consider 2-4 week strengthening period before filing. Significant RFE risk, but manageable.

---

### Profile D: BORDERLINE Case
- 0-1 pillars STRONG
- Multiple WEAK pillars

**Recommendation:** Consult immigration attorney before filing. May need 3-6 months of strengthening. High RFE risk.

---

### Profile E: WEAK Case
- All 3 pillars WEAK
- Multiple RED FLAGS

**Recommendation:** DO NOT FILE YET. Focus on building evidence. Consult attorney. Timeline: 6-12 months of preparation.

---

## Section 5: Next Steps by Profile

### If STRONG (Profile A)
- [ ] Compile supporting documentation
- [ ] Write petition draft
- [ ] Have attorney review before filing (recommended)
- [ ] File petition

**Timeline:** Ready to file within 2-4 weeks

---

### If MODERATE with Strong Majority (Profile B)
- [ ] Identify the weaker pillar
- [ ] Gather additional evidence for that pillar (2-3 weeks)
- [ ] Strengthen narrative around weak pillar
- [ ] Prepare for likely RFE focus area
- [ ] Compile documentation and file

**Timeline:** Ready to file within 2-4 weeks, prepare for RFE within 6 months

---

### If MODERATE Mixed (Profile C)
- [ ] Prioritize strengthening the weakest pillar
- [ ] Gather additional evidence (publications, awards, documentation)
- [ ] Formalize Proposed Endeavor with institutional support
- [ ] Address RFE vulnerabilities proactively in petition
- [ ] Consider attorney consultation

**Timeline:** Ready to file within 4-8 weeks

---

### If BORDERLINE (Profile D)
- [ ] Identify which pillar is strongest
- [ ] Focus 80% of effort on strengthening weakest pillar
- [ ] Consider delaying filing 3-6 months to strengthen case
- [ ] **Strongly recommend attorney consultation**
- [ ] Realistic timeline for strong case: 6-12 months
- [ ] If must file now, prepare for multiple RFEs

**Timeline:** 6-12 months to develop strong case

---

### If WEAK (Profile E)
- [ ] **DO NOT FILE**
- [ ] Assess which pillar is most feasible to strengthen
- [ ] Develop a 6-12 month plan to build evidence
- [ ] **Mandatory attorney consultation**
- [ ] Only file once case reaches MODERATE-to-STRONG
- [ ] If you must file now (urgent timeline), expect 50%+ RFE rate and prepare for potential denial

**Timeline:** 6-12 months minimum before filing is advisable

---

## Section 6: Red Flag Checklist

If you checked ANY of these, your case has significant vulnerability:

- [ ] No third-party evidence of accomplishment
- [ ] No published evidence of national importance or documented need
- [ ] Vague or missing Proposed Endeavor
- [ ] No institutional affiliation or resource commitment
- [ ] Major career pivot without clear justification
- [ ] Significant gaps or inconsistencies in your work history
- [ ] Work is entirely internal (not externally validated)
- [ ] Proposed Endeavor is disconnected from your proven expertise

**Action:** Address each red flag before filing if possible. Each unaddressed red flag increases RFE risk.

---

## Section 7: Attorney Consultation Triggers

**Consider consulting an immigration attorney if:**

1. Your case is BORDERLINE or WEAK
2. You have significant red flags
3. Your field is new or emerging (unclear national importance)
4. You have a complex career history
5. You're unsure about self-diagnosis
6. You want professional confidence before filing

**Attorney consultation cost:** $2,000-$5,000 for case evaluation and petition review (typical)

**DIY filing cost:** USCIS filing fee + preparation time

**Potential cost of weak petition:** RFE, denied petition, reapplication delay = lost time and opportunity cost

---

## Final Recommendation

**Use this decision tree to:**
1. Honestly assess your current case strength
2. Identify your strongest and weakest pillars
3. Develop a strengthening strategy if needed
4. Decide whether to file now or prepare first
5. Determine if attorney consultation is wise

**Remember:** Self-assessment has bias. If you're at the borderline, get a professional opinion. The cost of consultation is minimal compared to the cost of a weak petition.
