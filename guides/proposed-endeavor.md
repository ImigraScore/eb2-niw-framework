# Proposed Endeavor: How to Write a Plan That Passes Scrutiny

The Proposed Endeavor is your commitment. It's not a job description or a aspirational statement. It's your answer to the question: "What will you do in the United States if we waive the job offer requirement?"

USCIS will evaluate your Proposed Endeavor against Dhanasar's execution capacity standard. Your plan must be:
- **Specific** (Not "advance research"—what research?)
- **Realistic** (Can you actually accomplish this?)
- **Time-bound** (What's your timeline?)
- **Resource-identified** (Who will you work with? How will it be funded?)
- **Continuous** (Does it build on your proven track record?)

---

## What a Weak Proposed Endeavor Looks Like

**Example:**
"I will conduct research in artificial intelligence and contribute to the field through publications and innovation. I am committed to advancing the state of AI technology and training the next generation of researchers."

**Problems:**
- "Conduct research in AI" is too broad (What specific problem?)
- "Contribute through publications" has no target (How many? What venue?)
- "Advance the field" is vague (What does advancement look like?)
- "Train researchers" is added without evidence you've done this before
- No timeline
- No collaborators named
- No institutional affiliation mentioned

**RFE risk:** Very high. USCIS will ask for specifics.

---

## What a Strong Proposed Endeavor Looks Like

**Example:**
"I will research AI-driven diagnostic methods for rare immunological diseases at [University Hospital]. Over the next 24 months, I will (1) develop a machine learning model to identify rare disease biomarkers using existing patient datasets (12-month milestone), (2) publish results in a top-tier medical journal (18-month milestone), and (3) collaborate with [named collaborators] to prepare the model for clinical validation (24-month milestone). This work builds directly on my prior research and is supported by [institutional letter of support]."

**Why this works:**
- Specific problem (AI for rare disease diagnosis)
- Clear deliverables (model, publication, clinical validation prep)
- Realistic timeline (12-24 months for these outcomes)
- Institutional affiliation (university hospital)
- Continuity (builds on prior research)
- Resource commitment documented (collaborators, institutional support)

**RFE risk:** Low. Specific enough to evaluate; realistic enough to believe.

## How to Write Your Proposed Endeavor

### Step 1: Identify Your Core Contribution

What is the *specific problem* you'll solve or *specific area* you'll advance?

**Wrong:** "AI research"
**Right:** "Machine learning models for real-time translation in low-bandwidth environments"

**Wrong:** "Healthcare consulting"
**Right:** "Helping hospitals optimize emergency department patient flow through operations research"

### Step 2: Define Your Deliverables

What will you produce? Be concrete.

**Weak:** "I will publish papers and conduct research."
**Strong:** "I will publish 3-4 peer-reviewed papers in computational biology journals and present findings at [specific conferences]."

**Weak:** "I will develop tools for the community."
**Strong:** "I will release an open-source software library with accompanying documentation and tutorials, targeting 10,000+ developers in the fields of data science."

**Weak:** "I will advise on policy."
**Strong:** "I will contribute to 2-3 policy white papers on critical infrastructure cybersecurity, submitted to the Department of Homeland Security."

### Step 3: Set a Timeline

What's your 12-24 month plan? Be realistic.

**Example timeline:**
- Months 1-3: Literature review and methodology development
- Months 4-12: Data collection and initial analysis
- Months 13-18: Publication preparation
- Months 19-24: Dissemination and impact planning

**Why this format works:**
- Shows you've thought through the work sequentially
- Demonstrates understanding of what's realistic
- Gives USCIS a way to evaluate feasibility

### Step 4: Name Your Collaborators and Affiliation

Don't be vague. Name the institution or collaborators.

**Weak:** "I will work with other researchers and hope to find university affiliation."
**Strong:** "I will conduct this work as a Senior Research Scientist at [University Name], collaborating with [named professor] and [named researcher], who have committed to this project (see attached letters)."

**Why:** USCIS wants evidence your plan is grounded in reality, not speculation.

### Step 5: Explain Resource Access

How will you do this work? What resources do you have?

**Examples of resource statements:**
- "My employer has allocated $250,000 in research funding for this project"
- "I have secured a grant from [funding organization] in the amount of $X"
- "The university will provide lab space, equipment, and access to X+Y graduate students"
- "I am self-funding this initial phase through savings while seeking grant funding by month 6"

**Why:** USCIS wants to know this isn't a pipe dream.

### Step 6: Connect to Your History

Show how this Proposed Endeavor builds on your proven capability.

**Example:**
"For the past 7 years, I have published 15 papers on machine learning applications in medical imaging. My proposed work continues this line of research by focusing specifically on rare disease diagnosis, an area where I have developing expertise and growing publication record."

**Why:** This shows continuity, not a random pivot.

## Common Proposed Endeavor Mistakes

### Mistake 1: Too Much Ambition

**Weak:** "I will revolutionize the field of quantum computing by solving open problems in error correction and develop multiple new algorithms."

**Problem:** Too much for one person, even a very talented one. USCIS will question feasibility.

**Fix:** Focus on specific, achievable objectives for 12-24 months.

### Mistake 2: Vague Institutional Status

**Weak:** "I plan to conduct research and seek academic affiliation."

**Problem:** You don't have anything lined up.

**Fix:** Secure preliminary commitments before filing. Get a letter of support from an institution.

### Mistake 3: No Timeline

**Weak:** "I will publish papers, conduct research, and contribute to the field over time."

**Problem:** No way to evaluate feasibility or hold yourself accountable.

**Fix:** Include specific milestones and dates.

### Mistake 4: Disconnected from Your History

**Weak:** "I've been a software engineer for 10 years, and I plan to transition to biotechnology consulting."

**Problem:** No evidence you have relevant expertise.

**Fix:** Either explain the connection or accept the risk. If there's no connection, consider whether this is the right endeavor for your petition.

### Mistake 3: No Evidence of Institutional Support

**Weak:** "I will work with researchers at [University X] to conduct this project."

**Problem:** Just saying the name doesn't prove commitment.

**Fix:** Include actual letters of support or commitment from [University X].

## Proposed Endeavor Templates by Field

### For Researchers

"Over the next 24 months, I will conduct research on [specific problem] at [institution]. The project will produce [specific deliverables: publications, presentations, datasets]. I will collaborate with [named researchers] who have committed to this project. My work builds on [X years] of prior research in this area, as evidenced by [Y publications, Z citations]. This project is supported by [funding source and amount] and institutional affiliation with [institution]."

### For Practitioners/Consultants

"I will provide [specific service] to [target population] through [institution/organization]. Over 24 months, I will deliver [specific deliverables: reports, toolkits, case studies]. My work is grounded in [X years] of practical experience in [field], including [specific prior projects]. I will collaborate with [named organizations/individuals] who are committed to this initiative. The work is supported by [funding/institutional affiliation]."

### For Entrepreneurs/Product Developers

"I will develop [specific product/tool] addressing [specific problem] over the next 12-24 months. Key milestones include [specific deliverables by specific dates]. My prior work demonstrates capability to execute: [cite prior products/tools released]. I will work with [collaborators/team members] and am based at [institutional/company affiliation]. The project is funded through [source]."

## Red Flags in Proposed Endeavors

USCIS looks for these warning signs:

1. **Vagueness:** "Advance research" without specifics
2. **Overambition:** Scope that's unrealistic for one person in 24 months
3. **No institutional home:** "I'll figure out where to work"
4. **Disconnection from history:** Pivot without explanation or evidence of capability
5. **Passive voice:** "Research will be conducted" vs. "I will conduct"
6. **No timeline:** Ongoing indefinite work
7. **Speculation:** "I hope to find funding" instead of "I have secured"
8. **Generic field-level language:** "Advance healthcare" instead of "Improve diagnostic accuracy for [specific disease]"

---

## How to Strengthen a Weak Proposed Endeavor

If your initial draft is weak, here's the fix sequence:

**Step 1 (Quick):** Add specificity. Replace vague terms with concrete objectives.

**Step 2 (Medium effort):** Secure institutional support. Get a letter from a university, lab, or organization committing to host/support your work.

**Step 3 (Longer effort):** Identify funding. Secure grants, employer support, or demonstrate sufficient personal resources.

**Step 4 (Most important):** Connect to your history. Show how this work builds on proven expertise.

---

## Testing Your Proposed Endeavor

Ask yourself these questions:

1. **Could someone else understand exactly what I'm committing to?** (Clarity test)
2. **Could I realistically accomplish this in 24 months?** (Feasibility test)
3. **Is this what I've demonstrated capability to do?** (Credibility test)
4. **Do I have actual institutional/resource commitments?** (Grounding test)
5. **Would a skeptical evaluator believe I'm serious about this?** (Believability test)

If you answer "No" to any question, your Proposed Endeavor needs revision.

---

**Your Proposed Endeavor is where you convince USCIS you're not just qualified—you're committed. Make it specific, realistic, and grounded in reality.**
